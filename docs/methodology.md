# Methodology

Growth Marketing Jobs is designed to make growth-related roles easier to discover than they are on broad job boards.

The project uses public company job-board data and organizes roles into a focused index for growth marketing, performance marketing, CRO, SEO, lifecycle, analytics, and web growth work.

## Source-first approach

The preferred source is the company’s own job board or applicant tracking system page.

This matters because company job boards are usually closer to the original posting than scraped reposts, aggregator copies, or third-party job listings.

## Role classification

Roles are classified based on signals in the job title, job description, company context, and growth-channel language.

Common classification signals include:

- Growth
- Performance marketing
- Paid acquisition
- Paid search
- Paid social
- SEO
- Conversion rate optimization
- Experimentation
- Lifecycle marketing
- CRM
- Marketing analytics
- Web growth
- Demand generation
- Revenue marketing

The goal is not to include every marketing job. The goal is to include jobs where the work is clearly connected to measurable growth.

## Page quality principles

Programmatic pages should be useful, not just generated.

A page should generally have enough real inventory or enough explanatory value to deserve indexation. Thin pages, empty pages, and expired job pages should not be treated as successful SEO pages.

## Expired jobs

Job boards naturally create expired URLs.

The preferred behavior is:

- Keep the URL useful when possible.
- Clearly indicate when a role is no longer active.
- Avoid presenting expired roles as live.
- Link users toward similar active roles.
- Use appropriate indexation controls when a page no longer deserves to appear in search.

## Search engine goals

The project is built to be discoverable by search engines, but the indexing goal is not to create as many URLs as possible.

The goal is to create a clean, crawlable, useful index where the important pages are:

- Accessible
- Canonical
- Internally linked
- Backed by real inventory
- Clear about what they represent
